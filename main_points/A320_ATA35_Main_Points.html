Core Concept

ATA 35 is emergency survival→It is used for depressurization, smoke, fumes, and toxic environments.not normal use
Three separate technologies→Crew oxygen is gaseous, passenger oxygen is chemical, and portable systems are mobile emergency equipment.
Follow the oxygen path→Storage → regulation → distribution → mask → control mode.big idea
👨‍✈️

Flight Crew Oxygen

High-pressure reusable system→Oxygen is stored in a cylinder, regulated down, and distributed to crew masks.
Core flow→Cylinder → regulator → low-pressure supply valve → manifold → stowage boxes → masks.
Most important safety system→Crew oxygen keeps pilots functional during smoke, fumes, or depressurization.critical
🧯

Oxygen Cylinder

High-pressure storage→The crew cylinder stores oxygen at high pressure and feeds the regulator.
Manual valve and pressure gauge→Used for servicing, isolation, and pressure verification.
Burst disc protects overpressure→The cylinder has overpressure protection to prevent dangerous rupture.protected
⚙️

Pressure Regulator

Most critical crew component→The regulator converts high cylinder pressure into usable low pressure.critical
Feeds ECAM pressure data→Pressure transmitter/regulator data supports cockpit indication and warnings.
Internal relief protection→Protects the low-pressure side if regulation goes abnormal.
Failure compromises the crew system→If the regulator fails, downstream crew oxygen supply can be affected.
🟡

Discharge Indicator

External visual clue→Green indicates normal. Yellow indicates system discharge or overpressure event.inspection clue
Yellow disc needs immediate inspection→Do not reset your brain to “normal” if the discharge indicator shows yellow.
Possible causes→Overpressure, regulator issue, or discharge event.
🔘

Low Pressure Supply Valve

Controlled by CREW SUPPLY switch→ON allows oxygen flow. OFF isolates the crew oxygen system.
Maintenance isolation point→Useful for leak control, servicing, and safe troubleshooting.isolation
Switch command is not the whole story→Verify actual downstream pressure and mask flow.
🧩

Distribution Manifold

Splits oxygen to the crew→The manifold distributes regulated oxygen to the crew stations.
Low-pressure warning is downstream→REGUL LO PR points to the low-pressure/distribution side after regulation, not simply cylinder pressure.important
Leak checks follow the manifold path→Start upstream and work downstream to isolate the pressure loss.
😷

Stowage Box Logic

Mask removal opens flow→Removing the mask opens the valve, shows OXY ON, and activates the microphone.mask position
Flow indication matters→A yellow cross or flow indicator confirms oxygen is actually flowing.
Manual reset after stowing→Stowing the mask does not always reset everything automatically.
🎚️

Crew Mask Modes

NORM mode→Provides a mix of ambient air and oxygen during normal demand use.
100% mode→Provides pure oxygen when required.
EMERGENCY mode→Positive-pressure oxygen protects against smoke/fumes entering the mask.smoke mode
Altitude logic can change oxygen use→At high altitude, the system can automatically shift toward 100% oxygen logic.
🧪

Passenger Oxygen

Chemical oxygen generation→Passenger oxygen is generated chemically, not stored as a large oxygen supply.very different
One-time emergency system→Once activated, the generator cannot be stopped and must be replaced after use.
Limited oxygen duration→Designed to support emergency descent, not long-term breathing supply.
🔥

Chemical Generator Hazard

Activated by mask pull→The passenger pulls the mask, the lanyard activates the chemical generator.
Gets very hot→Chemical oxygen generators are a serious heat and fire-risk item after activation.hazard
Cannot be stopped→Once the reaction starts, oxygen generation continues until the generator is spent.
⬇️

Mask Deployment Logic

Automatic cabin-altitude deployment→Masks deploy automatically at high cabin altitude, or manually by MASK MAN ON.
Two-stage sequence→Altitude switch opens latch → masks drop → passenger pulls mask → generator starts.critical
Mask drop is not oxygen flow→Oxygen starts when the mask is pulled and the generator activates.
⚠️

Passenger Fault Logic

FAULT can be electrical→A passenger oxygen fault can involve latch power logic, not the oxygen generator itself.MX trap
Door/latch problems are common→Check latch release, door condition, and generator state.
Expired or fired generators matter→Always verify generator status and serviceability.
🚻

Lavatory Oxygen

Different design→Lavatory oxygen can use a pressurized bottle instead of a chemical generator.
Lanyard activation→Pulling the lanyard starts continuous oxygen flow until empty.continuous flow
Do not assume cabin generator logic→Lav oxygen troubleshooting is not always the same as passenger service unit oxygen.
🚒

Portable Oxygen

PBE protects against smoke/fire→Protective Breathing Equipment is not just oxygen; it protects the user in smoke environments.smoke protection
Portable cylinders support mobile use→Used for passenger medical needs or emergency support away from fixed outlets.
Check expiration and seals→Portable equipment often fails due to expiration, missing seals, or serviceability status.
🚨

Maintenance Safety — Highest Priority

Never contaminate oxygen systems→Oxygen with oil, grease, or hydrocarbons can create a fire or explosion hazard.critical rule
Use clean tools and proper plugs→Keep oxygen fittings clean, capped, and protected during maintenance.
Avoid rapid pressurization→Pressurize correctly and treat high-pressure oxygen with respect.
🔧

Common Failures & Line Reality

Crew system common faults→Low pressure, leaks, regulator issues, transducer faults, and low-pressure switch faults.
Passenger system common faults→Latch faults, generator expired/fired, door problems, and electrical release issues.
Portable system common faults→Expired units, seal damage, low pressure, or missing equipment.inspect status

🔧 Pro Troubleshooting Flow

1. ECAMCheck DOOR/OXY page, cylinder pressure, and REGUL LO PR.
2. IdentifyCrew, passenger, lav, or portable system.
3. Pressure PathCylinder → regulator → LP valve → manifold → mask.
4. ComponentsRegulator, supply valve, sensors, switches, and stowage box.
5. Passenger FaultsCheck latch, door, generator status, and mask activation.
