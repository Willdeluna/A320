Core Concept

Doors are structural + safety systems→They seal pressure, carry loads, allow access, and support evacuation.big idea
Not just access panels→Door systems combine structure, locking, sensors, logic, indications, and safety systems.
Real model→Structure → lock mechanism → sensors → logic → indications → safety systems.MX map
🧩

Door Types

Passenger / crew doors→Plug-type doors with slide systems.
Emergency exits→Smaller plug-type exits with different handling and slide logic.
Cargo doors→Hydraulic, outward-opening doors with electrical and hydraulic control.
Service/access/cockpit doors→Different structural and security functions depending on door type.
🧱

Plug-Type Door Physics

Passenger doors move inward first→Then up, outward, and forward to clear the fuselage opening.very important
Cabin pressure helps keep door closed→Pressure pushes the plug door into its frame.
Pressure is part of the safety design→The latch locks the door, but pressure loads it into the stops/frame.pressure logic
🏗️

Door Structure & Load Path

Door is part of pressure vessel→Skin, frame, longerons, and stop fittings carry pressurization loads.structure
Pressure loads transfer through stops→Cabin pressure loads the door into stop fittings and fuselage structure.
Misalignment matters→Rigging, stops, seals, and fit can create leaks or indication faults.
🔒

Locking Mechanism

Overcenter mechanical safety lock→Locking hooks, shaft, link rods, and bellcranks secure the door mechanically.critical
Unlock sequence→Handle moves shaft, shaft rotates, hooks disengage, door becomes free to move.
Lock sequence→Door closes, shaft rotates, hooks engage, overcenter lock secures the door.
Must be fully locked before pressurization→Partial lock or bad sensor feedback can create ECAM/warning issues.
🔁

Door Movement System

Movement is mechanically controlled→Lifting lever, support arm, and guide arms move the door through its travel path.not hydraulic
Door moves up to clear stops→The door must lift before it can move outward safely.
Rigging affects feel and indication→Stiff movement or bad alignment can cause latch/sensor mismatch.
⚠️

Door Damper & Emergency Cylinder

Controls and assists door movement→Dampers slow normal movement; emergency cylinders assist rapid opening.
Emergency assist can open violently→Gas assist can rapidly drive the door open during emergency operation.hazard
Disarm during maintenance→Always respect door assist and slide arming safety before work.
🧿

Door Seal

Pressure-activated sealing→The inflatable rubber seal is helped by cabin pressure, not just mechanical compression.pressurization
Seal condition affects leaks→Cuts, flattening, contamination, or bad seating can create pressure/noise complaints.
Seal troubleshooting includes rigging→A “seal leak” can be seal condition or door fit/alignment.
📡

Proximity Sensors

Most door faults are sensor/indication faults→Sensors monitor door position, lock status, handle position, and slide mode.common fault
Data path→Sensors → SDAC → FWC → ECAM.
Indications come from logic→ECAM does not “see the door” directly; it sees processed sensor data.
📊

Indications & Warnings

ECAM DOOR page is primary→Green means closed, amber means open, and amber X means invalid data.start here
Cabin indications matter→SLIDE ARMED lights and CABIN PRESS warnings protect crew and passengers.
Warnings prevent unsafe opening→Pressure warning logic helps stop doors from being opened when pressurized.
🚨

Cabin Pressure Warning Logic

Prevents opening a pressurized door→Warnings trigger when door opening would be unsafe due to cabin pressure.critical safety
Logic uses multiple conditions→Slide status, engine status, and cabin pressure logic can combine for warnings.
Do not ignore pressure warnings→A pressurized door event can be violent and dangerous.
🛝

Escape Slide System

Armed + door open = slide deploys→If the door is armed and opened, the slide deploys automatically.critical
Disarmed + door open = no deployment→Disarming changes the physical connection so the slide stays with the door.
Slide system is safety equipment→Treat arming levers, girt bar, reservoir, and inflation parts carefully.
🔗

Girt Bar

Physical slide deployment link→The girt bar connects the slide to the aircraft floor when armed.key component
Armed mode→Girt bar attached to floor, so opening the door pulls the slide out.
Disarmed mode→Girt bar attached to door, so opening the door does not deploy the slide.
💨

Slide Inflation

Deployment sequence→Door opens → slide pulled out → lanyard pulls valve → inflation starts.
Gas plus aspirator→Reservoir gas starts inflation, while aspirator pulls in ambient air for rapid volume.fast inflate
Manual inflation backup→Red PULL handle is used if automatic inflation fails.
🪟

Overwing Exits

Different from passenger doors→Overwing exits are plug-in hatches manually removed by passengers/crew.different system
Slide stored separately→Overwing slide may be stored in the wing fairing, not in the hatch itself.
Hatch removal triggers slide logic→Latch pin and release mechanism control automatic deployment.
📦

Cargo Doors

Very different system→Cargo doors are hydraulic, electrically controlled, and outward opening.hydraulic
Yellow hydraulic system powers operation→Electric pump/Yellow system pressure moves actuators and door mechanism.
Mechanical + hydraulic integration→Locking shaft, hooks, vent door, drift pins, and actuators all coordinate.
🌬️

Vent Door Safety

Vents pressure before opening→The vent door releases pressure before the cargo door opens.safety critical
Same safety principle as plug doors→Never fight pressure; remove pressure first.
Vent logic faults can inhibit operation→If venting or feedback is invalid, control logic can prevent door movement.
💧

Cargo Door Hydraulics

Hydraulic pressure moves the cargo door→Yellow system pressure powers actuators for opening/closing.
Dual actuators share load→Actuators move the door and include internal mechanical locking features.
Actuators lock mechanically→The door is not held only by hydraulic pressure.mechanical lock
⚡

Cargo Door Electrical Control

Logic controls hydraulic action→LGCIU, relays, and selector valves coordinate cargo door operation.ATA 32 link
Safety interlocks prevent unsafe operation→Door locked, aircraft not on ground, or invalid logic can inhibit movement.
Check logic before hardware→No movement may be a valid inhibit, not a failed actuator.
🔧

Common Failures & Line Reality

Most common faults→Proximity sensors, slide armed/disarm faults, cargo door valve issues, hydraulic issues, and rigging/misalignment.
Structural failure is rare→Most write-ups are indication, sensor, rigging, slide, valve, or hydraulic control issues.line MX gold
Troubleshoot indication first→ECAM DOOR page and sensor status tell you where to start.

🔧 Pro Troubleshooting Flow

1. ECAM DOORCheck closed/open/invalid indications.
2. IdentifyCabin door, cargo door, overwing exit, or slide system.
3. StatusLocked? Closed? Armed? Pressurized?
4. SensorsProximity sensors, handle, lock, slide mode feedback.
5. MechanismLatch, hook, shaft, alignment, seal, damper.
6. CargoYellow hydraulics, valves, actuators, LGCIU/interlocks.
