Core Concept

APU = self-contained auxiliary gas turbine→It produces electrical power and pneumatic bleed air for the aircraft.big idea
Complete engine system→It has its own control, fuel, oil, air, start, ignition, and protection logic.
Single shaft drives everything→The turbine powers compressor, load compressor, generator, and accessory gearbox.one shaft
🧩

System Architecture

Three big sections→Power section, load compressor, and accessory gearbox.
Power section is the core engine→Air compression, combustion, and turbine power happen here.
Load compressor makes bleed air→Separate from the core compressor, it supplies aircraft pneumatic users.
Accessory gearbox distributes mechanical power→It drives fuel, oil, generator functions and integrates the oil reservoir.
⚡

System Purpose

Electrical power source→The APU generator can supply aircraft electrical power on ground or as backup.
Bleed air source→APU bleed supports engine start, air conditioning, and backup pneumatic supply.
Electrical power has priority→If load must be managed, electrical output is prioritized over pneumatic bleed load.priority
🔥

APU Engine Core

Centrifugal compressor→Compresses air for the combustion process.
Reverse-flow annular combustor→Burns fuel/air mixture to create turbine-driving energy.
Turbine drives the system→Turbine energy drives the compressor, load compressor, and gearbox.power source
⚙️

Accessory Gearbox

Power distribution hub→The gearbox mechanically drives fuel, oil, and generator-related systems.gearbox = hub
Oil reservoir integration→The gearbox also houses or integrates the APU oil reservoir.
Gearbox faults can affect multiple systems→Fuel, oil, and generator issues can trace back to gearbox-driven functions.
🧠

ECB — APU Brain

ECB acts like APU FADEC→It controls start sequence, fuel flow, speed, IGV, valves, and shutdown.controls everything
Sensor-driven logic→Pressure, temperature, speed, and aircraft signals feed ECB decisions.
BITE and fault storage→ECB stores faults and supports maintenance troubleshooting through CFDS/MCDU.
🚀

APU Start Sequence

Start is RPM-based→Each start event happens at specific APU speed milestones.very important
Typical sequence→Starter ON → fuel/ignition ON → acceleration → starter OFF → ignition OFF → APU AVAIL.
Every step depends on RPM→No acceleration or wrong speed feedback can stop the sequence and create shutdown logic.
🔋

Starting System

Starter accelerates to self-sustaining speed→The starter motor, contactors, and clutch get the APU rotating for lightoff.
Starter disengages automatically→Once the APU accelerates enough, the ECB commands starter disengagement.auto cutout
Starter faults create no-start or slow-start symptoms→Check power, contactors, clutch, and speed feedback.
⚡

Ignition System

Ignition only needed during start→After flame becomes self-sustaining, ignition is no longer required.start only
ECB controls ignition→The ignition unit and igniter plug are commanded during the start window.
No flame triggers protection→If ignition/fuel/air do not produce flame, the ECB shuts the APU down.
⛽

Fuel System

Fuel flow controls engine speed→The ECB meters fuel through the FCU to control acceleration and running speed.speed control
Flow path→Aircraft fuel → FCU → flow divider → nozzles.
Primary and secondary fuel logic→Fuel scheduling changes between starting and acceleration/running phases.
🎛️

FCU & Flow Divider

FCU meters fuel→Fuel pump, filters, and torque motor valve respond to ECB command.critical
Flow divider splits fuel→It routes fuel between primary and secondary paths for start and acceleration.
Flow divider faults affect starts→Hung start, no start, or poor acceleration can point to flow divider/check valve issues.MX clue
🛢️

Oil System

Oil protects the APU engine→Lubrication, cooling, and wear detection protect gears, bearings, and rotating parts.critical
Main components→Reservoir, lube module, filters, oil cooler, sensors, and chip/wear monitoring.
Oil faults cause protective shutdown→Low pressure, high temperature, or low level can make the ECB shut the APU down.
🛡️

Oil Protection Logic

Oil failure means shutdown→The APU cannot safely run without correct oil pressure, temperature, and quantity.protective
ECB monitors oil system→Oil sensors feed shutdown and fault logic.
Protective shutdown is not optional→Protection overrides load demand to prevent engine damage.
🌬️

APU Air System

Load compressor produces bleed air→APU pneumatic output is generated by the load compressor.
IGV controls airflow→Inlet guide vanes adjust airflow into the compressor based on demand.demand control
Load control valve controls bleed output→LCV opens when APU is available and bleed is selected.
Surge control valve prevents compressor stall→SCV protects airflow stability, especially when load conditions change.
⚠️

Surge Control Valve

Prevents compressor surge/stall→The SCV helps keep the load compressor stable when airflow is low or changing.critical
Fail-safe open→Open position protects the compressor from damaging surge conditions.
Air faults can be valve/IGV faults→No bleed or poor bleed can be LCV, IGV, SCV, or ECB command logic.
⚡

Electrical Output

APU generator supplies aircraft power→Look at voltage, frequency, load percentage, and AVAIL status.primary output
Electrical priority over pneumatic→The system protects electrical output before allowing excessive bleed load.
Generator faults may not be core faults→Check generator, control, load, and indications before blaming the APU engine.
🚪

Inlet System

Air intake flap opens with MASTER ON→The APU needs inlet airflow before start is allowed.
Must be open for start→If the flap is not open or feedback is invalid, start protection can stop the APU.FLAP NOT OPEN
Check actuator and feedback→Inlet faults can be command, actuator, sensor, or ECB logic.
🛑

Protective Shutdown Logic

Protection overrides everything→Overspeed, overtemp, low oil pressure, no flame, no acceleration, reverse flow, or sensor failures can shut it down.critical
Immediate shutdown can skip cooldown→Serious protection faults shut down immediately to protect the aircraft/APU.
Common shutdown does not mean core failure→Many shutdowns are sensor, control, fuel, oil, or valve logic actions.
🧊

Normal Shutdown Logic

Remove load first→Normal shutdown unloads the APU before fuel is cut off.
Cooldown protects turbine→If bleed was used, cooldown helps prevent heat damage.heat protection
Fuel off ends operation→After cooldown/unload logic, the ECB shuts off fuel and stops the APU.
📊

ECAM APU Page

Primary troubleshooting page→APU page shows speed, EGT, bleed pressure, generator load, and AVAIL status.start here
Identify the phase→Was the problem during start, running, load application, or shutdown?
Use CFDS/MCDU history→Last-leg faults and BITE data help separate sensor logic from actual mechanical faults.
🔧

Common Failures & Line Reality

Common fault sources→Temperature, pressure, and speed sensors; fuel system faults; starter issues; ECB faults; valves and actuators.
Core engine failure is less common→Most APU write-ups are control, sensor, fuel, oil, or protection logic before true core failure.line MX gold
Troubleshoot by phase→No start, hung start, shutdown, no bleed, no generator power, or no AVAIL all point down different paths.
