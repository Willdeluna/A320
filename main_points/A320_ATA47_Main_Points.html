Core Concept

ATA 47 prevents fuel tank explosion→It reduces oxygen concentration in the fuel tank using Nitrogen Enriched Air.prevention
Not fire suppression→The goal is to make the tank ullage non-flammable before ignition can occur.
Lower oxygen means no combustion→The system displaces oxygen so the center tank environment stays below flammability risk.big idea
🧩

System Architecture

Two main subsystems→CSAS prepares bleed air, and IGGS removes oxygen to generate nitrogen-enriched air.
Basic flow→Bleed air → CSAS → IGGS → fuel tank.
Heavy ATA 36 dependency→No correct bleed air means no reliable fuel tank inerting.ATA 36 link
🔁

How It Works

Step 1: bleed air supply→Air is taken from engine or APU bleed sources.
Step 2: CSAS conditions air→Air is cooled, pressure-controlled, and prepared for the IGGS.
Step 3: IGGS separates oxygen→ASM creates NEA for the tank and OEA that is vented overboard.
Step 4: tank becomes inert→NEA enters tank ullage and oxygen is displaced through the vent system.non-flammable
🌬️

CSAS — Air Conditioning for IGGS

CSAS prepares air before separation→It removes ozone, cools air, and controls pressure before air reaches IGGS.critical
Main components→Isolation valve, ozone converter, heat exchanger, hot air bypass valve, and sensors.
IGGS needs correct air conditions→Too hot, too much pressure, or contaminated air can shut the system down.
🎛️

CSAS Control

Controlled by CCU→The CSAS Controller Unit opens valves, monitors temp/pressure, and protects the air supply.
Isolation valve starts airflow→Air only flows when system conditions allow the isolation valve to open.
Protection closes the system→Too hot or too high pressure can make the CCU shut the valve.fail safe
🧪

IGGS — Main System

IGGS removes oxygen from air→It creates nitrogen-enriched air for the tank and sends oxygen-enriched air overboard.main function
Monitors air quality and performance→Sensors verify pressure, temperature, oxygen concentration, and valve behavior.
Automatic safety system→It operates without crew control and protects itself if conditions are not right.
🫧

ASM — Air Separation Module

Heart of the system→The ASM uses membrane fibers to separate oxygen and nitrogen.core component
Produces NEA→Nitrogen-enriched air goes to the fuel tank to reduce oxygen level.
ASM contamination hurts performance→Oil, dust, or filter problems can reduce nitrogen generation effectiveness.
🧰

IGGS Components

Key components→Isolation valve, D-ULPA filter, pressure sensors, temperature sensor, oxygen sensor, ASM, and DFSOV.
D-ULPA filter protects the ASM→It removes contamination like oil and dust before air reaches sensitive membrane fibers.filter matters
Clogged filter reduces performance→Reduced flow or contaminated air can create inerting faults without ASM failure.
📊

Oxygen Sensor

Verifies system effectiveness→The oxygen sensor measures oxygen percentage in the nitrogen-enriched air.huge
High oxygen means shutdown→If oxygen concentration is too high, the system can shut down to fail safe.
Bad sensor can look like bad inerting→Sensor faults can trigger shutdown even if airflow hardware is healthy.
🚪

DFSOV

Controls NEA flow→The Dual Flow Shut-Off Valve controls nitrogen-enriched air flow into the tank.flow valve
Isolates system from tank→DFSOV closes to protect the tank/system during faults or shutdown.
Multiple flow modes→Low, medium, and high flow support different flight phases and tank conditions.
🛫

Flow / Flight Phase Logic

Cruise uses steady flow→System flow stabilizes tank oxygen concentration during normal flight.
Descent may need increased flow→Tank ullage volume and pressure change with altitude, so flow demand can change.adaptive
System changes with aircraft conditions→FTIS is not one fixed-flow pipe; it adapts automatically.
🧠

ICU — System Brain

IGGS Controller Unit controls the system→ICU manages valves, sensors, flow rate, protection logic, and BITE.brain
Performs health monitoring→It watches performance and commands shutdown when system conditions are unsafe.
Use BITE for troubleshooting→MCDU system tests help identify sensor, valve, and controller faults.
🛡️

Protection Logic

System fails safe→High temp, high pressure, low pressure, or high oxygen concentration can shut it down.fail safe
Isolation valves normally closed→Valves open only when the system is operating and conditions are safe.
Backflow protection matters→Check valves prevent fuel or vapor contamination from entering the IGGS side.
⚙️

Automatic Operation

No normal crew control→The system operates automatically based on bleed availability and aircraft conditions.autonomous
Fault response closes valves→On fault, isolation valve, DFSOV, and related valves close to protect the aircraft.
ECAM/STATUS/CFDS guide maintenance→FUEL INERTING SYS FAULT and maintenance messages point you to the stored fault path.
⚠️

Maintenance Safety

Low oxygen hazard→System air can be oxygen-poor, creating a hypoxia risk around opened access areas.life critical
Wait after opening access→Allow time for the area to ventilate before working near inerting system access.
Treat NEA like a hazard→It is not toxic smoke, but low oxygen atmosphere can still incapacitate you.
🔧

Common Failures & Line Reality

Common fault sources→Temperature sensors, pressure sensors, oxygen sensor issues, DFSOV failures, isolation valve issues, filter contamination, and bleed air problems.
ASM failure is less common→Check air supply, conditioning, sensors, filters, and valves before blaming the membrane module.don’t shotgun ASM
ATA 36 problems become ATA 47 problems→If bleed air is wrong, CSAS/IGGS performance will suffer.

🔧 Pro Troubleshooting Flow

1. ECAM / CFDSCheck FUEL INERTING SYS FAULT and stored maintenance messages.
2. Bleed SourceVerify ATA 36 source, pressure, and temperature availability.
3. CSASCheck temperature, pressure, isolation valve, CCU, and conditioning.
4. IGGSCheck oxygen sensor, pressure sensors, D-ULPA filter, ASM, and DFSOV.
5. BITE TestUse MCDU system test to confirm valve/sensor/controller fault path.
