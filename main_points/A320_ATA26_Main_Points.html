Big Picture — How It Works

Two main jobs: detect and extinguish→Detection finds the fire. Extinguishing kills it quickly.
Active protection uses detection and extinguishing→Loops, smoke detectors, fire bottles, and automatic discharge logic.
Passive protection is built into the aircraft→Fire containment, shielding, separation, and design features slow the threat down.
Different zones use different systems→Engines/APU use fire and overheat loops. Cargo, lav, and avionics use smoke or heat detection.
🔥

Engine Fire System

Each engine has two detection loops→Loop A and Loop B help prevent false alarms and keep redundancy.
Normal logic uses both loops→Both loops must detect fire to trigger normal fire warning logic.AND logic
If one loop fails, the system can go single-loop→Still protected, but with less redundancy.OR logic
Fire response = isolate then extinguish→Shut off fuel, bleed, hydraulics, and generator path before bottle discharge.
⚙️

Engine Fire Pushbutton

Pressing FIRE pb isolates the engine→Fuel off, bleed off, hydraulic supply isolated, and generator disconnected.huge
The pushbutton arms the bottles→You cannot discharge the extinguisher bottle until the system is armed.
Two bottles per engine→Bottle 1 first, then Bottle 2 if the fire indication remains.
The fire pb is not just a light→It is the big isolation and arming control for the engine fire system.
🧯

Extinguishing Process

Wait about 10 seconds before discharge→Lets the engine spool down so the agent works better.
If fire remains after about 30 seconds, use second bottle→That is the standard backup shot logic.
Extinguishing agent is Halon under pressure→Fast suppression with a direct bottle discharge path.
🌡️

Fire Detection System

Loops use pressurized gas inside sensor tubes→Heat raises pressure and causes the detector to respond.
Two switch functions matter→Alarm switch means fire. Integrity switch means loop health.
A leaking loop is an integrity fault→Do not confuse a loop leak with an actual fire condition.troubleshooting trap
Detection can respond to average heat or local hot spots→Overheat and fire flame conditions are both part of the logic.
🧠

FDU Logic

FDU is the system brain→It reads loop signals and sends fire/overheat warnings.
Dual channel logic adds redundancy→Each loop has its own monitoring channel.
Faults are stored for troubleshooting→Use BITE/maintenance messages before shotgun replacing parts.use BITE first
Bad loops can be isolated automatically→The system keeps protection available when one side has a problem.
⚠️

Warning Logic

Fire warning occurs when both loops detect fire→That is the normal high-confidence warning path.
Fire plus fault combinations can still trigger warnings→The system protects against losing warning capability.
Single loop fault is a caution, not a fire warning→Aircraft may still be protected by the remaining loop.MEL mindset
Both loops failed is a major problem→No reliable detection means the protection layer is seriously degraded.
🔧

Bottles & Hardware

Engine has two bottles, APU has one→Engines get a backup shot. APU does not.
Pressure switch monitors bottle condition→A DISCH indication means bottle pressure is low, discharged, or leaking.
Relief disc protects against overpressure→If the disc bursts, the bottle is unserviceable.inspection clue
🔥

APU Fire System

Same basic idea as engine fire protection→Detection loops, warning logic, and a bottle discharge path.
In flight, extinguishing is manual→Crew action is required to discharge the bottle.
On ground, APU fire can auto-extinguish→If the crew does not act, the system can protect the aircraft automatically.ground auto
Only one APU bottle→There is no second shot available for the APU.
📦

Cargo Fire System

Smoke detection gives the warning first→The system alerts before extinguishing is commanded.
No automatic cargo discharge→The pilot must push the DISCH pushbutton.manual discharge
Single-shot or ETOPS dual capability→Longer flights may need extended fire suppression protection.
Ventilation shuts off when smoke is detected→Containing the fire is part of controlling the threat.
🚻

Lavatory Fire System

Lav trash-bin extinguishing is fully automatic→No crew action is needed for the bottle to discharge.
Heat melts the plug and releases agent→The system targets trash-bin fires quickly.
Temperature indicator shows overheating history→Good maintenance clue during lav fire bottle inspections.easy points
