Big Picture — How It Works

AC power is primary→AC runs most systems. DC power is secondary and comes from TRs converting AC.
Main power sources→IDG 1, IDG 2, APU GEN, and external power.
Two AC sides normally stay separate→AC BUS 1 and AC BUS 2 normally run independently until the system needs a tie.bus logic
⚙️

Power Source Logic

Priority starts with own engine IDG→Each side prefers its own generator first.
Then external power, APU, opposite IDG→The system automatically picks the best available source.
No paralleling of generators→The system prevents two sources from feeding the same bus at the same time.very important
BTCs auto-connect when needed→If one side is lost, bus tie contactors let the opposite side support both.
⚡

Generators / IDG

IDG makes constant 400 Hz power→It converts variable engine speed into stable aircraft AC power using CSD logic.
Each engine drives its own generator→GEN 1 normally powers BUS 1. GEN 2 normally powers BUS 2.
IDG disconnect is one-way in flight→You can disconnect it, but reconnect/reset is ground only.don’t forget
Common faults are oil temp or pressure→Fault light usually means the IDG is protecting itself from damage.
⚠️

IDG Protection Logic

Oil temp above 185°C→Warning condition. Disconnect is required.
Oil temp above 200°C→Auto disconnect to prevent damage.self protect
Low oil pressure below 140 psi→Creates an IDG fault condition.
Line takeaway→If you see an IDG fault, act immediately or the unit may self-protect.
🔌

External Power

AVAIL light means power quality is good→Without AVAIL, external power will not connect.
GAPCU monitors the source→Voltage, frequency, phase, overload, and other parameters must be acceptable.
Bad parameter equals disconnect→If quality drops, the aircraft protects the bus.ground ops gold
External power has priority over APU→When both are available, external power is used first.
🔋

DC System

Three TRs convert AC to 28V DC→TR1, TR2, and ESS TR keep DC buses powered.
Automatic backup between TRs→If one TR fails, another takes over automatically.
ESS TR feeds essential DC during failures→It comes online automatically when the logic needs it.essential power
🔋

Batteries

Two batteries around 24V each→Used for APU start and emergency power.
BCL protects the battery→Prevents overheat, overcharge, and deep discharge.
Normally isolated in flight→Batteries are not continuously powering the system during normal flight.
🚨

Emergency Power

Loss of both AC buses deploys RAT→The RAT deploys automatically when both AC buses are lost.
RAT drives the emergency generator→Hydraulic power from the blue system drives emergency generation.
Only essential buses are powered→Emergency generation is for flight-critical systems only.critical system
🔄

Static Inverter

Battery DC becomes AC→The static inverter converts DC battery power into AC power.
Useful when generators are not available→It supports low-speed and emergency situations.
Bridge during RAT transition→Think of it as short-term backup AC while emergency logic catches up.backup AC
🎛️

Electrical Control Panel

BUS TIE AUTO is normal→Allows automatic bus tie logic to connect buses when needed.
AC ESS FEED controls essential source→Manual backup to select which bus feeds essential power.
GEN pushbutton connects generator to bus→Generator must be ON and healthy before it supplies the bus.
⚠️

Load Shedding

Galley loads go first→Automatic load shedding removes non-essential load during overload.ops reality
Single generator means automatic load reduction→The system prevents overloading the remaining source.
🔧

Maintenance / Troubleshooting Gold

Most problems are not the generator→Usually contactors, control units, sensors, or bus logic.
Missing power? Follow the path→Check source, contactor, and priority logic before shotgunning parts.
CFDS/BITE stores faults→Always check fault history before troubleshooting.check BITE first
TR faults need reset after clearing→They will not always auto-recover just because the fault condition is gone.
