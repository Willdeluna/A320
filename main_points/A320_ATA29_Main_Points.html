Core Concept

Hydraulics = force system→ATA 29 transmits power using pressurized fluid, not just fluid flow.big idea
Fluid pressure becomes movement→Pressure moves actuators, brakes, gear, steering, and flight controls.
Think pressure path→Pump → pressure → manifold → user → return.
🟢

System Architecture

Three independent systems→Green, Yellow, and Blue systems provide redundancy.
No fluid mixing→Systems are independent. The PTU transfers power, not fluid.exam point
Normal pressure is about 3000 psi→Pressure is the main indicator of hydraulic system health.
⚙️

Power Sources

Green: Engine 1 EDP→Primary source is ENG 1 engine-driven pump, with PTU backup.
Yellow: Engine 2 EDP + electric pump→Yellow has strong backup and ground-operation capability.
Blue: electric pump + RAT→Blue system is electrically powered and emergency-capable through RAT.true redundancy
🔁

PTU — Power Transfer Unit

Transfers power, not fluid→Green and Yellow stay hydraulically separate while sharing mechanical power.huge
Activates with pressure difference→About 500 psi differential can trigger PTU operation.
Works both directions→Green can help Yellow, and Yellow can help Green.
Barking dog sound can be normal→PTU noise alone does not mean a fault.
🪂

RAT — Emergency Source

RAT is last-resort power→It supplies Blue hydraulic pressure and emergency electrical power.last resort
Spring deployed→It can deploy when commanded, so treat the area with respect.
Ground retraction only→Retraction is hydraulic and done on the ground.
✈️

Hydraulic Users

Hydraulics power major loads→Flight controls, brakes, landing gear, steering, and thrust reversers.
Loads are distributed→No single system carries every critical load by itself.
Priority valves protect flight controls→During low pressure, non-essential loads can be shut off first.priority logic
🛢️

Reservoirs

Reservoirs store fluid→They provide quantity indication and low-level warning.
Reservoirs are pressurized→Air pressure helps prevent pump cavitation.
Air pressure matters→Low air pressure can be just as important as low fluid quantity.MX clue
⚙️

Pumps

EDPs are primary sources→Engine-driven pumps create the main pressure for Green and Yellow.
Electric pumps support backup and ground ops→Yellow and Blue electric pumps provide non-engine hydraulic power.
Pump output matches demand→Variable displacement pumps adjust output instead of blasting full flow all the time.
Pump protection matters→Overheat can shut a pump down. Low pressure creates ECAM warning logic.
🔋

Accumulators

Hydraulic battery→Accumulators smooth pressure and provide short-term power.
Nitrogen pre-charge→The gas side stores energy so pressure can be released quickly when needed.
🧩

Manifolds, Filters & Valves

Manifolds are system hubs→HP manifolds distribute pressure. LP manifolds return fluid.
Filters protect the system→HP and return filters catch contamination before it spreads.
Relief valves protect from overpressure→Check valves prevent reverse flow and relief valves prevent damage.
Clog indicators are early warnings→Do not ignore filter bypass or clog indications.
🌡️

Monitoring

Pressure is the main health clue→Low pressure drives ECAM and MASTER CAUT logic.primary clue
Temperature protects the system→Overheat can damage fluid, seals, pumps, and components.
Leak measurement helps maintenance→Useful for isolating flight-control sections during leak troubleshooting.
📊

ECAM Indications

HYD ECAM page is the first stop→Look at pressure, quantity, temperature, pumps, and reservoir status.start here
Common messages→SYS LO PR, RSVR LO LVL, and OVHT are key warnings.
Warnings may be phase-inhibited→Some warning behavior changes during high-workload phases like takeoff.
🔧

Maintenance Reality

Most faults are indications or pump logic→Sensors, pressure/temp indications, pumps, reservoirs, and filters are common repeat-write-up areas.
Yellow electric pump has special logic→Cargo door operation can affect hydraulic logic and isolate flight controls.
Always identify the system first→Green, Yellow, or Blue determines the pressure source, users, backup path, and likely fault path.line MX mindset
🔧 Troubleshooting Flow
1. ECAM HYDCheck pressure, quantity, temp, and pump status.
2. Identify SystemGreen, Yellow, or Blue — do not troubleshoot blind.
3. Check SourceEDP, electric pump, PTU, or RAT path.
4. Follow PressureSource → manifold → user → return.
5. Verify ProtectionPriority valves, filters, relief/check valves, and leak isolation.
