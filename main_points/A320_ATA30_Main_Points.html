Core Concept

ATA 30 is heat management→The goal is to keep critical aircraft areas ice-free and visibility usable.big idea
Protects aerodynamics and engine performance→Ice changes airflow, blocks sensors, and can create engine ingestion problems.
Uses bleed air and electric heat→Large surfaces use hot air. Sensors and smaller systems use electric heating.
🧩

System Architecture

Main subsystems→Wing anti-ice, engine anti-ice, probe heat, window heat, rain removal, and water/waste anti-ice.
Different zones use different protection→The system is not one big heater. Each area has its own logic and failure clues.
ATA 30 depends heavily on ATA 36→Bleed air faults can look like anti-ice faults.bleed link
❄️

Wing Anti-Ice

Protects slats 3, 4, and 5→Wing anti-ice protects selected leading-edge slats, not the whole wing.partial protection
Uses engine bleed air→Each engine supplies hot air for its own wing side.
Hot air goes through piccolo tubes→Bleed air travels through the valve, ducts, piccolo tubes, slats, then overboard.
Ground operation is limited→Ground testing is limited to prevent overheating the slats.
⚙️

WAI Control & Faults

Electrical command, pneumatic movement→The valve is electrically commanded and pneumatically actuated.key logic
Auto-adjusts bleed source→The system can use IP bleed normally and HP bleed when needed.
FAULT light clues→Valve disagreement, low/high pressure, or electrical problems can trigger faults.
Most WAI faults are valve or pressure related→Don’t jump straight to ducts before checking command, valve position, and pressure.
🌀

Engine Anti-Ice

Protects engine intake lip→This protects the engine ingestion area and is safety-critical.
Uses hot compressor air→Engine anti-ice uses hot air from the compressor section.
EAI valve controls operation→The butterfly valve position determines whether anti-ice air is flowing.
Anti-ice ON reduces available thrust→Expect a performance penalty when engine anti-ice is used.performance hit
🧠

EAI Logic

Controlled by ENG ANTI-ICE PB→The cockpit command flows through engine control logic.
EIU and FADEC are involved→Engine anti-ice affects FADEC logic and engine performance limits.huge
Valve position matters→Commanded ON does not help if the valve does not actually move.
📡

Probe Heat

Critical flight safety system→Protects pitot tubes, static probes, AOA sensors, and TAT probes.critical
Three independent channels→Captain, F/O, and standby channels provide redundancy.
PHC controls heating→The Probe Heat Computer adjusts heating based on aircraft phase.
Ground heating is reduced→Auto logic reduces heating on ground and changes behavior in flight.
⚠️

Probe Heat Hazards

Probes get extremely hot→Treat probes like burn hazards during ground maintenance.
Depower correctly→Incorrect CB or power handling can create unwanted heating conditions.MX hazard
Sensor heat faults matter→Loss of probe heat can affect air data reliability.
🪟

Window Heat

Electrically heats flight deck windows→Windshields and side windows are heated for anti-ice and visibility.
WHC controls temperature→The Window Heat Controller manages power and protects the glass.
Low on ground, high in flight→Heating power adapts to operating conditions.
Overheat protection cuts power→The system protects glass integrity if temperature gets too high.glass protection
🌧️

Wipers & Rain Repellent

Two independent wiper systems→Each side has its own basic, reliable visibility backup.
Modes are OFF, SLOW, FAST→Simple control with auto park to protect the blades.
Blade condition matters→Bad blades can scratch or leave poor visibility during rain.
Rain repellent is supplemental→Manual spray helps visibility, but it is not the primary rain-removal system.
🚰

Water / Waste Anti-Ice

Drain masts are heated→Electrical heat prevents external ice buildup around drains.
Temperature-controlled system→Heater logic cycles based on temperature and system control.
Often overlooked→Drain mast heat faults can hide behind CIDS or CFDIU indications.hidden system
👀

Ice Detection Concept

Pilot/visual awareness matters→Ice risk is often managed by crew observation and system use.
Not every aircraft uses automatic detection→Know the aircraft configuration before assuming automatic ice detection logic.
🔧

Maintenance Reality

Most faults are control or valve related→WAI/EAI valve issues, pressure sensing, electrical heaters, PHC, and WHC faults dominate.
Bleed and electrical faults can look similar→Always identify whether the system uses hot air or electric heat before troubleshooting.
Main question: Is heat getting there?→Then ask whether the heat is being controlled correctly.line MX mindset
🔧 Troubleshooting Flow
1. Identify SystemWAI, EAI, probe heat, window heat, wipers, or drain mast heat.
2. Check ECAMLook at BLEED, ANTI-ICE, pressure, valve, and temperature clues.
3. Find Heat SourceBleed air for large surfaces. Electric heat for sensors and visibility.
4. Follow ControlPB command, controller, valve position, heater status, and protection logic.
5. Verify OutputHeat source → valve/controller → distribution → protected component.
