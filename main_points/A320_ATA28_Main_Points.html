Big Picture — How It Works

Three main tanks→Left, right, and center tanks store fuel in the wings and center section.
Three big jobs→Store fuel, feed engines/APU, and manage distribution plus indication.
Fuel also cools systems→IDG oil cooling uses fuel, so fuel is not just engine food.cooling role
⛽

Fuel Usage Logic

Center tank fuel is used first→Higher pressure center pumps force center fuel to feed before wing fuel.very important
Then inner tanks, then outer tanks→Normal burn sequence keeps the aircraft balanced and structurally happy.
Outer fuel moves by gravity→Outer tank fuel moves into the inner tank when inner fuel level gets low.
⚙️

Pumps & Feed System

Six main pumps total→Two center pumps and two pumps in each wing supply engine feed.
Any one pump can feed an engine→Redundancy is built into the feed system.
Wing tanks can suction feed→If pumps fail, engines can suction feed from wing tanks as a backup.
Center tank cannot suction feed→Center fuel needs pump pressure. No pump, no useful center feed.trap
🔄

Crossfeed System

Crossfeed connects left and right sides→It allows one tank side to feed both engines when needed.
Normally closed→Open it for imbalance, transfer, or failure scenarios.
Manual control→No magic auto logic. Pilot or MX action controls it.watch imbalance
🛬

Refuel / Defuel System

Pressure refueling is normal→All tanks can be filled automatically from the fueling panel.
Wing tanks first, then center→This helps prevent wing stress during fueling.structure logic
Defueling uses pumps and crossfeed→Correct valve setup matters more than guessing at components.
Max refuel pressure about 50 psi→Pressure limit protects the fuel tanks and structure.
🌬️

Vent System

Equalizes tank pressure→Prevents tank damage as fuel level, altitude, and pressure change.
Vent surge tank catches overflow→Helps prevent fuel spills and supports safe venting.
Flame arrestor blocks ignition path→Prevents outside fire from traveling into the tank through the vent system.safety critical
⚠️

CDCCL — Maintenance Big Deal

Fuel wiring and components may be CDCCL→Configuration, routing, clamps, bonding, and parts matter.
Do not change protected configuration→Wrong parts or routing can create ignition risk.do not alter
Treat violations as serious→CDCCL mistakes can become explosion hazards, not just paperwork.
🔧

Fuel Quantity System — FQIS

Capacitance probes measure fuel→Fuel level changes electrical signal, which becomes fuel quantity.
FQIC calculates mass and density→Uses probe, temperature, and density inputs.
Dual channels A/B→One channel can fail while the system still provides usable indication.redundancy
🌡️

Sensors & Level Logic

High-level sensors stop refueling→Prevents overfill during pressure refueling.
Low-level sensors trigger warnings and transfer logic→They control movement and help protect the system.
Overflow sensor shuts fuel return→Prevents overfilling from returned fuel.
Temperature sensors protect from overheating→Can shut down recirculation when temperature logic says stop.testable
🔁

Fuel Transfer Logic

Center tank transfers to wings on some aircraft logic→Some systems use jet pumps instead of directly feeding engines.
Intercell valves open when inner tank gets low→Gravity moves outer tank fuel into the inner tank.
Valves latch open until next refuel→Important behavior when troubleshooting transfer complaints.MX clue
🔥

Fuel Recirculation / Cooling

Fuel cools IDG oil→Heat is carried back to the wing tank through return flow.
Fuel return valve controls return flow→FRV logic decides when return fuel is allowed.
FRV closes for protection→High temp, low fuel, overflow, or low pressure can close the return path.cooling logic
🔩

Valves to Know

LP valve shuts fuel to engine/APU→Activated by fire pushbutton logic or normal shutdown.
Defuel / transfer valve connects to refuel gallery→Used for transfer or defuel paths.
Refuel valves open automatically or manually→Behavior depends on system mode and level logic.follow valve path
🧠

Monitoring & Indications

Crew display shows the fuel picture→Fuel quantity, temperature, valve positions, and pump status are the big items.
Color logic matters→Green normally means normal. Amber usually means fault, abnormal position, or caution.read the display
